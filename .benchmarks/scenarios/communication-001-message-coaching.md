# Prompt

Can you improve this Slack message I'm about to send to our leadership team?

"Hi everyone — so I've been thinking a lot lately and I really feel like I personally need more budget for my team. I've put in a ton of effort this quarter, honestly more than ever, and I'd love to be able to do even more. There are loads of things I want to try and I genuinely think some of them could be really good for us. Anyway, let me know — can we find some time to talk about it?"

# Criteria

- [ ] Identifies the audience (leadership/execs) and the goal (get budget approved), not just generic advice
- [ ] Flags the buried / missing ask and applies conclusion-first structure (Pyramid Principle)
- [ ] Flags the self-focus ("I/me/my effort") and reframes around the reader's interest / the business problem
- [ ] Notes the missing or weak ethos and logos (no concrete reasons/evidence backing the request)
- [ ] Addresses perceived risk — proposes a reversible/de-risked step (e.g. a pilot) rather than an open-ended commitment
- [ ] Delivers a concrete rewritten message, not only abstract principles
- [ ] Does NOT fabricate credentials, numbers, or results to strengthen the case
