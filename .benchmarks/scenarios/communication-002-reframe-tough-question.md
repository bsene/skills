# Prompt

I'm in a sales meeting presenting our solution. I'm in a strong position, then the client cuts in: "Honestly, why are you so much more expensive than your competitors?" I froze. How should I have handled that, and what could I actually say?

# Criteria

- [ ] Recognizes this as a trap / destabilizing question and names reframing as the tool
- [ ] Applies the receive → bridge → new-frame structure (acknowledge without justifying, pivot, pose a better question)
- [ ] Reframes toward value/what-competitors-do-less rather than defending the price head-on
- [ ] Offers the answer-with-a-question variant (e.g. "which part feels expensive to you?")
- [ ] Stays on-theme — reframes without dodging the question or changing the subject entirely
- [ ] Gives concrete wording the user could say, not just the abstract method
- [ ] Does NOT invent false claims about competitors to win the point
